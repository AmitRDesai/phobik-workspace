Chapter 8:
Getting Trapped in Your Own Fear

Fear during a flight is often fueled by our imagination rather than actual danger. Our minds create worst-case scenarios, replacing reality with terrifying 'what-ifs'.

When the seatbelt sign illuminates, it's easy for the mind to leap to conclusions. A perfectly normal operational procedure becomes a signal of impending doom in the anxious mind. This is the 'fear trap' – where physiological arousal is misinterpreted as proof of danger.

self_improvement

Grounding Exercise

Use the 5-4-3-2-1 technique: see 5 things, feel 4, hear 3, smell 2, and taste 1. This anchors you back to reality.

By actively engaging your senses, you interrupt the cycle of anxious thoughts. You are reminding your brain that right now, in this moment, you are safe in your seat.

"Worry does not empty tomorrow of its sorrow, it empties today of its strength."
