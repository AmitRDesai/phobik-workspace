Chapter 15: Building Tolerance Without Avoidance

Avoidance is the most common response to fear. When we feel anxious about flying, our instinct is to avoid the situation entirely or rely on safety behaviors to get through it. However, avoidance prevents us from learning that the situation is actually safe.

psychology

Tolerance Matters More Than Comfort

The goal is not to immediately feel comfortable, but to build tolerance to the discomfort. Each time you face the fear without avoiding it, your tolerance grows.

Think of tolerance as a muscle. The more you exercise it by staying in uncomfortable situations without escaping, the stronger it gets. Eventually, what once felt intolerable becomes just another manageable experience.
