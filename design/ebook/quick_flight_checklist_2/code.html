Chapter 13:
Panic is not always Danger

When you're in the air and turbulence hits, your body might react as if it's in a life-or-death situation. But a core principle of overcoming flight anxiety is understanding this distinction:

Commitment ends panic

Panic feeds on hesitation. It thrives in the space where you are deciding whether you should try to escape or fight. On a plane, escape isn't an option. Once you commit entirely to the fact that you are on this flight until it lands, the fuel for panic begins to dry up.

Acceptance is not defeat. It is the active, powerful choice to stop fighting the reality of your situation.
